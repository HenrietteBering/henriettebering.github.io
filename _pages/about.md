## About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher studying social inequality, with a focus on 
children and families. My work examines how families reproduce 
and transmit inequalities across generations, using quantitative methods.

My research sits at the intersection of stratification research,  sociology 
of the family, and sociogenomics. I am interested in how social contexts and 
genetic predispositions jointly shape children's cognitive and educational 
development, and how gene-environment interplay contributes to the 
intergenerational reproduction of educational and cognitive inequalities.

Currently, I am working in the DFG-funded project *The Interplay of Social Contexts and 
Genetic Potentials: Cognitive and Educational Development in the Early Life 
Course*, based at the University of Bremen.
