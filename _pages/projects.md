## The interplay of social contexts and genetic potentials: cognitive and educational development in the early life course
This project explores how the family of origin shapes children's cognitive and educational development through both social and genetic influences. Building on sociogenomics research, it investigates the interplay of social contexts and genetic potentials in shaping developmental pathways, particularly in three areas: remote learning during the COVID-19 crisis, children’s extracurricular activities, and educational inequality in rural versus urban areas. The projects will use newly available data sources and innovative methods to move beyond the family environment as the main social context shaping inequality in the early life course. We will combine genetically informed designs such as twin studies, or extended family designs which rely on differences in the degree of genetic relatedness, and on polygenic scores constructed from genome-wide association studies (GWAS). In doing so, we explore mechanisms behind the interplay of social and genetic (dis-)advantages, investigating how they foster early achievement, or how they might hinder children’s development.

Funding: Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft (DFG)

Duration: 15 September 2024 - 15 September 2027
